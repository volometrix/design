Meeting Effectiveness

Your goal is for x% of your meetings to be considered high quality.

Percent of time in high quality meetings: (i) X%

nPeer average:X%

On hover/click (i) button: A meeting’s quality is determined by its MQI, the Meeting Quality Index, which uses meeting components like the duration of the meeting, number of attendees, level of engagement, number of declines/double books, and number of levels present to rate the meeting. Learn more. (link to FAQs)

Meeting Effectiveness Components (i)

On hover/click (i) button: These components are not mutually exclusive, so their totals may exceed 100%.

Duration is less than 1 hour On hover/click: Everyone’s time counts, making larger meetings more costly. Additionally, more people make it harder to focus, and can limit the meeting’s productivity. Learn more. (link to FAQs)

Less than 6 attendees On hover/click: Scheduling longer meetings increases the meeting’s cost and the possibility of conversations running off topic. Be frugal with your time and others’. Learn more. (link to FAQs)

100% engagement On hover/click: If meeting attendees are sending email and getting other work done during the meeting, it lowers the level of engagement. Learn more. (link to FAQs)

No declines/double booked attendees On hover/click: There are other ways to keep your peers in the loop than inviting everyone to every meeting. Only invite people who are absolutely necessary to the meeting to avoid high levels of declines and double bookings. Learn more. (link to FAQs)

Less than two levels present On hover/click: Before you attend or organize a meeting, make sure that the attendees won’t create redundancies. It’s unnecessary to have more than two levels of employees present at most meetings. Learn more. (link to FAQs)

x axis = percent of time in meetings
